# Title And Angle

Use this reference when selecting a topic angle, generating titles, or rewriting the opening promise.

## Corpus Signals

- 644 directory titles were analyzed.
- 408 titles used first or second person pronouns such as 我, 你, or 我们.
- 198 titles used question or challenge structures.
- 141 titles signaled relationship or intimacy conflict.
- 89 titles signaled money, work, class, or consumption pressure.
- 76 titles used a number, percentage, age, income, or money anchor.
- 42 titles used a colon structure to name an object and then deliver a judgment.

Treat these as directional signals, not quotas.

## Angle Selection

Convert a neutral topic into one of these reader conflicts:

- Relationship: `I want love, but I am being controlled, consumed, ignored, or humiliated.`
- Money and class: `I want dignity, but money exposes how little choice I really have.`
- Work and growth: `I want progress, but the rules reward obedience, performance, or self-betrayal.`
- Family: `I want to love them, but love is being used as debt, control, or guilt.`
- Public event: `Everyone is judging the event, but the more painful truth is closer to ordinary life.`
- Reader story: `Many private embarrassments reveal one public pattern.`
- Social judgment: `A polite norm is hiding a cruel transaction.`

Choose the lane that creates the most personal consequence for the target reader.

## Title Molds

Generate at least 10 title candidates before choosing. Use different molds.

1. Direct address
   - `你不是在[表层问题]，你是在[深层困境]`
   - `我为什么不再[社会期待]`
   - `我们这一代人，最怕的不是[表层恐惧]`

2. Question or challenge
   - `为什么[普通行为]，会毁掉[高价值关系/人生结果]？`
   - `凭什么[某类人]，要替[另一类人]买单？`
   - `到底什么样的[对象/关系]，才值得[付出/留下/放弃]？`

3. Number or money anchor
   - `[数字]个细节，暴露了[关系/职场/家庭]里的真相`
   - `月薪[数字]的人，最怕的不是穷`
   - `花[金额]买来的不是[物品]，是[身份/安全感/自由]`

4. Colon verdict
   - `致[对象]：[一句边界判断]`
   - `[热点人物/事件]：真正可怕的不是[表层争议]`
   - `[关系对象]：不是我不懂事，是你太会装无辜`

5. Reversal
   - `不是[常识判断]，而是[更刺痛的真相]`
   - `[被夸赞的品质]，正在毁掉你`
   - `你以为[行为]是爱，其实是[控制/逃避/交易]`

6. Emotional object
   - `一个[日常物品]，让我看清了[关系真相]`
   - `[具体场景]之后，我终于不想讨好任何人了`
   - `[一句普通话]，杀死了[重要关系/自尊/希望]`

## Title Quality Checks

A strong title should pass at least four checks:

- It names a specific reader, role, or situation.
- It implies a conflict, not just a theme.
- It contains a consequence: loss, humiliation, awakening, freedom, money, love, or dignity.
- It can be understood in one glance.
- It has a viewpoint, not just curiosity.
- It is original and not a near-match to a source title.

## Opening Promise

After choosing a title, write one sentence that answers: `读完这篇，读者会获得什么情绪许可？`

Examples of promises:

- Permission to stop apologizing.
- Permission to admit a relationship is unfair.
- Permission to care about money without shame.
- Permission to reject fake maturity.
- Permission to laugh at a painful social rule before judging it.
